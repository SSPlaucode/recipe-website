Butter Chicken

Murgh Makhani — the dish that conquered the world

Prep Time 15 min
Cook Time 30 min
Serves 4
Difficulty Medium

Tender marinated chicken in a velvety tomato-cream sauce — this is the dish that introduced Indian cuisine to the world. Rich, mildly spiced, and deeply satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 700g Chicken (boneless thighs)
  • 1 cup Yoghurt (full-fat)
  • 2 tbsp Lemon juice
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 1 tsp Cumin powder
  • 1 tsp Garam masala
  • 2 tbsp Ginger-garlic paste
  • 3 tbsp Butter
  • 2 Medium onions
  • 4 Large tomatoes
  • 15 Cashew nuts
  • 1/2 cup Fresh cream
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • To taste Salt

Instructions

01
Marinate the Chicken

In a bowl, combine yoghurt, lemon juice, Kashmiri chilli powder, cumin powder, garam masala, ginger-garlic paste, and a generous pinch of salt. Add chicken pieces, coat well, and marinate for at least 30 minutes (overnight is better).

02
Grill or Pan-Sear the Chicken

Heat a grill pan or heavy skillet over high heat. Cook the chicken pieces in a single layer for 5–6 minutes per side until charred and cooked through. Set aside.

03
Make the Makhani Base

Melt 2 tbsp butter in a pan. Add sliced onions and fry until golden. Add tomatoes and cashews, cook for 10 minutes until soft. Let cool, then blend to a smooth puree.

04
Build the Sauce

Melt remaining butter in the same pan. Pour in the blended puree through a strainer (optional, for silkier texture). Add 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli, garam masala, and sugar. Cook on medium heat for 8 minutes, stirring often.

05
Combine and Finish

Add the cooked chicken to the sauce. Simmer for 5 minutes. Lower the flame and stir in fresh cream. Taste and adjust salt. Finish with a small knob of butter swirled in off the heat.

06
Serve

Garnish with a swirl of cream and a pinch of dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi). Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed basmati rice.

Chef's Tips

  • Kashmiri chilli gives the beautiful red colour without excessive heat — don't substitute regular chilli powder
  • Blending cashews into the sauce adds body without needing excessive cream
  • Charring the chicken in step 2 adds smokiness that defines authentic murgh makhani
  • Add a pinch of crushed kasuri methi at the end — it transforms the flavour
