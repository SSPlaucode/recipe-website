Dal Makhani

The Punjabi classic that rewards patience

Soak Time8 hrs
Cook Time2–3 hrs
Serves4–5
DifficultyEasy

Slow-cooked whole black lentils and kidney beans, finished with a generous amount of butter and cream. The longer it simmers, the better it gets.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Whole black lentils (sabut urad)
  • 1/4 cup Red kidney beans (rajma)
  • 3 tbsp Butter
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1 Bay leaf
  • 2 Large onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-garlic paste
  • 3 Medium tomatoes, pureed
  • 1.5 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp Garam masala
  • 1/2 cup Fresh cream
  • To taste Salt

Instructions

01
Soak Overnight

Wash the black lentils and kidney beans thoroughly. Soak in plenty of cold water for 8 hours or overnight. This significantly reduces cook time.

02
Pressure Cook the Lentils

Drain and rinse the soaked lentils. Add to a pressure cooker with 3 cups water and a pinch of salt. Cook for 4–5 whistles until completely soft. The lentils should mash easily between your fingers.

03
Prepare the Makhani Base

Heat butter and oil in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add bay leaf, then onions. Fry on medium heat for 15–18 minutes until deeply golden — this step is not to be rushed. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook 2 minutes.

04
Add Tomatoes and Spices

Add the pureed tomatoes, Kashmiri chilli, and coriander powder. Cook until the oil separates from the masala, about 10 minutes, stirring regularly.

05
Slow Simmer

Add the cooked lentils along with their water to the pot. Mix well, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to the lowest flame. Partially cover and simmer for at least 1 hour, stirring every 15 minutes. The dal should thicken and the colour deepen.

06
Finish with Cream

Stir in garam masala and fresh cream. Simmer for another 10 minutes. Finish with a knob of cold butter stirred in off the heat. Serve with rice or naan.

Chef's Tips

  • The ideal dal makhani simmers for 6–8 hours on a very low flame — this is how dhaba restaurants achieve that depth of flavour
  • Never skip the soaking step — it makes the lentils creamier when cooked
  • A small piece of charcoal for the dhungar (smoke) method will add a restaurant-style smoky note
  • Leftovers are even better the next day
