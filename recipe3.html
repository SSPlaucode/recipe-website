Gulab Jamun

India's most beloved festive sweet

Prep Time20 min
Cook Time20 min
Serves5–6
DifficultyMedium

Soft, pillowy dumplings made from reduced milk solids, fried to a deep amber, and soaked in a fragrant syrup of rose water and cardamom. Pure indulgence.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Milk powder (full-fat)
  • 2 tbsp All-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 tsp Baking soda
  • 2 tbsp Ghee (melted)
  • 3–4 tbsp Warm milk (to knead)
  • — Oil or ghee for frying
  • 1.5 cups Sugar
  • 1.5 cups Water
  • 4 Cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 tbsp Rose water
  • Few strands Saffron (optional)

Instructions

01
Make the Sugar Syrup

Combine sugar and water in a wide pot. Bring to a boil, stirring to dissolve. Add crushed cardamom, rose water, and saffron strands. Simmer for 5 minutes until slightly sticky. Keep warm on very low heat.

02
Make the Dough

Mix milk powder, flour, and baking soda in a bowl. Add melted ghee and mix until it resembles breadcrumbs. Add warm milk one tablespoon at a time, kneading gently until a soft, smooth dough forms. Do not overwork it.

03
Shape the Jamuns

Divide the dough into 18–20 equal portions. Roll each into a smooth ball between your palms with gentle pressure — no cracks on the surface. Slightly oval shapes are traditional.

04
Fry Low and Slow

Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan to medium-low (around 150°C). Add jamuns in batches — do not crowd. Fry for 8–10 minutes, turning constantly, until they reach a deep mahogany brown. High heat will brown the outside before cooking the inside.

05
Soak and Rest

Remove jamuns and immediately drop into the warm sugar syrup. They will expand slightly as they soak. Cover and let rest for at least 2 hours — overnight is ideal. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Chef's Tips

  • The dough must be soft — if it cracks when you roll, add a few more drops of milk
  • Low frying temperature is the most critical factor: too hot and the outside burns before the inside cooks
  • Warm syrup absorbs better than cold — keep it on the lowest flame while frying
  • For stuffed jamuns, press a tiny piece of dried fruit or pistachio into the centre before rolling
