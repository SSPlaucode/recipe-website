Hyderabadi Biryani

Dum-cooked, layered, unforgettable

Marinate2 hrs
Cook Time60 min
Serves5–6
DifficultyHard

Fragrant basmati rice layered over spiced marinated chicken, sealed and slow-cooked in the dum method. Every spoonful is a mixture of textures and aromas.

Ingredients

  • 700g Chicken (bone-in pieces)
  • 2 cups Basmati rice (aged)
  • 1 cup Yoghurt
  • 2 Large onions (for birista)
  • 2 tbsp Ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp Biryani masala
  • 1 tsp Red chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric
  • Few strands Saffron in warm milk
  • 3 tbsp Ghee
  • Handful Fresh mint leaves
  • Handful Fresh coriander
  • 2 Bay leaves, cardamom, cloves
  • To taste Salt

Instructions

01
Make Birista (Crispy Onions)

Thinly slice onions. Deep fry in oil over medium heat until golden brown and crispy. This is the defining flavour of Hyderabadi biryani. Drain and set aside. Reserve the oil.

02
Marinate the Chicken

In a bowl, mix yoghurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, chilli powder, turmeric, salt, half the birista, half the mint, and 2 tbsp of the birista oil. Add chicken and marinate for at least 2 hours (overnight gives deeper flavour).

03
Par-Cook the Rice

Soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a boil. Add whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, cloves). Cook rice until 70% done — it should still have a chalky centre. Drain and spread on a tray.

04
Layer the Biryani

In a heavy-bottomed pot (with a tight lid), spread the marinated chicken as the base layer. Layer half the par-cooked rice on top. Scatter half the remaining birista, mint, coriander, and drizzle saffron milk and ghee. Add remaining rice and repeat the topping.

05
Dum Cooking (Sealed Steam)

Seal the pot tightly with a lid — use atta (wheat dough) to seal the edges for authentic dum. Place on a tawa (flat pan) over low heat for 35–40 minutes. This indirect heat ensures even cooking. Do not open mid-cook.

06
Rest and Serve

Let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before opening. Use a large spoon to gently mix from the sides, bringing the chicken layer up. Serve with raita and mirchi ka salan.

Chef's Tips

  • Aged basmati (at least 1 year) is non-negotiable — fresh rice turns mushy in dum cooking
  • The birista is the soul of Hyderabadi biryani — don't rush or substitute
  • 70% cooked rice is the target — it finishes cooking in the dum steam
  • A tawa under the pot prevents the bottom layer from scorching
