Chole Bhature

Delhi's iconic street food combination

Soak Time8 hrs
Cook Time45 min
Serves4
DifficultyMedium

Spicy, tangy chickpea masala paired with pillowy, puffed deep-fried bread. A combination that defines North Indian street food and Sunday mornings across Delhi.

Ingredients

  • —For the Chole
  • 1.5 cups Dried chickpeas (kabuli chana)
  • 2 Large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 Medium tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp Chole masala powder
  • 1 tsp Amchur (dry mango powder)
  • 1 tsp Red chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric
  • 1 Teabag (for colour)
  • —For the Bhature
  • 2 cups All-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup Fine semolina (sooji)
  • 1/2 cup Yoghurt
  • 1/2 tsp Baking soda
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • — Oil for frying

Instructions

01
Soak and Pressure Cook Chickpeas

Soak dried chickpeas overnight in plenty of water. Next day, drain and pressure cook with a teabag, salt, and 3 cups water for 5–6 whistles until completely tender. The teabag gives authentic dark colour. Discard the teabag.

02
Make the Chole Masala

Heat 3 tbsp oil in a kadai. Add onions and fry until deep golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook 2 minutes. Add tomato puree and all dry spices. Cook until oil separates, about 10 minutes.

03
Combine and Simmer

Add the boiled chickpeas with about 1 cup of their cooking water. Mix well, mashing a few chickpeas against the side of the pan to thicken the gravy. Simmer for 15–20 minutes until the masala coats the chickpeas. Add amchur for tartness.

04
Prepare the Bhature Dough

Mix flour, semolina, baking soda, sugar, and salt. Add yoghurt and knead into a soft, slightly sticky dough. Add a teaspoon of oil. Rest covered for 30 minutes minimum — this makes the bhature puff better.

05
Roll and Fry Bhature

Divide dough into 8 balls. Roll each into an oval about 6–7 inches long. Heat oil in a deep pan to 180°C. Slide one bhatura in — it should puff up within seconds. Press gently with a spoon to help it puff evenly. Fry until light golden (about 1 min per side). Drain on paper.

06
Serve Immediately

Bhature must be served hot — they deflate as they cool. Plate with the chole, a slice of raw onion, green chilli, pickle, and a wedge of lemon. Never keep bhature waiting.

Chef's Tips

  • The teabag trick is the secret to authentic dark chole — don't skip it
  • Resting the bhatura dough is non-negotiable for proper puffing
  • Oil temperature is critical for bhature — too cool and they absorb oil, too hot and they brown without puffing
  • Chole tastes significantly better made 2–3 hours ahead and reheated before serving
